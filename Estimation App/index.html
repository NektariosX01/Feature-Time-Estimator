Feature Time Estimator

Confidence Interval: the confidence interval is calculated as 80% to 120% of the adjusted time estimate. The adjusted time estimate takes into account the feature size, complexity, and experience level of the development team, and is calculated as follows:

  • The base estimate is determined by the feature size: XS=2 days, S=4 days, M=8 days, L=16 days, XL=32 days.
  • The complexity multiplier is determined by the complexity of the feature: Simple=1, Moderate=2, Complex=4.
  • The experience multiplier is determined by the experience level of the development team: Novice=2, Intermediate=1.5, Expert=1.
  • The adjusted time estimate is the product of the base estimate, the complexity multiplier, and the experience multiplier.

The confidence interval is a way to show the potential range of values for the time estimate, taking into account the uncertainty in the inputs and the factors that affect the development process. It is calculated from the adjusted time estimate T as follows:

  • The lower bound of the confidence interval is 80% of T.
  • The upper bound of the confidence interval is 120% of T.

For example, if the adjusted time estimate is 10 days, then the Confidence Interval would be:

  • Lower bound: 0.8 * 10 = 8 days
  • Upper bound: 1.2 * 10 = 12 days

So, the confidence interval for this estimate would be 8 to 12 days.
